SONGPREP: A PREPROCESSING FRAMEWORK AND END-TO-END MODEL FOR FULL-SONG STRUCTURE PARSING AND LYRICS TRANSCRIPTION

Abstract

Currently, Artificial Intelligence Generated Content (AIGC) is a highly popular research direction. Song generation, as one of its branches, has garnered increasing attention. How- ever, data has become a significant barrier to the development of this field. Despite the existence of a large amount of song audio, Converting these song files into data suitable for training generation models requires substantial labeling efforts and costs. To address this issue, we propose SongPrep, a pipeline for automatic song data preprocessing. This framework is designed to automate functions such as music source separation, structure analysis, and lyric recognition, ultimately generating structured data that can be directly used for training song generation models. In addition, we used data cleaned by SongPrep to train an end-to-end model for full song structure parsing and lyrics transcription, named SongPrepE2E. To evaluate the performance of model, we introduce a validation dataset named SSLD-200 (Song Structure and Lyric Dataset), which contains accurate and detailed manual annotations. The experiments conducted by SSLD-200 demonstrate that the proposed frameworks can produce structure information with a low Diarization Error Rate (DER), and lyric text with a low Word Error Rate (WER). It indicates that the data generated by SongPrepE2E can be utilized for training the music generation system directly, resulting in music compositions generated by our music generation system that closely resemble those created by humans.

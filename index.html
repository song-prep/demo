Abstract

Recent advances in audio-based generative language models have greatly propelled AI-powered lyric-to-song generation. However, such paradigms often suffer from hallucination, where models produce content misaligned with the input lyrics, compromising musical coherence. To address this, we introduce a reinforcement learning framework based on three preference optimization methods, which are Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), Proximal Preference Optimization (PPO), and Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO). First, we construct a preference dataset by comparing original lyrics with phonemes recognized from generated music. DPO then operates off-policy to increase the likelihood of positive token generation, reducing Phone Error Rate (PER) by 7.4% and indirectly mitigating hallucinations. From another perspective, based on the preference dataset, a reward model is trained for scoring generated token sequences by PER in on-policy PPO and GRPO. Through iterative on-policy sequence sampling and reward scoring, models are optimized via reward maximization and KL-divergence constraints, resulting in alleviation of hallucinations by 4.9% and 4.7% decrease in PER. Experimental results demonstrate our methods significantly suppress hallucinations while preserving musicality, as confirmed by subjective evaluations. This work provides a systematic solution to hallucination control in lyric-to-song generation, with transferable reinforcement learning frameworks that enable music style following, musicality enhancement, etc. It provides new avenues for future research in music generation.
